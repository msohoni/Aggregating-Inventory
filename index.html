NovaCare Medical Service Parts

case questions

NovaCare Medical Systems sells and services diagnostic devices used by hospitals and outpatient clinics. Its installed base requires rapid replacement of medical service parts. Today the same part families are stocked in five Regional Distribution Centers (RDCs): Northeast, Southeast, Midwest, Texas, and West Coast.

NovaCare is considering a National Distribution Center (NDC) in Indianapolis. For each part family × region cell, students may choose one stocking mode: keep stock at the RDC, move full stocking responsibility to the NDC, or use SS → NDC, where local cycle stock remains regional and safety stock is pooled centrally. RDC-to-RDC movement is not allowed.

The full case brief, data, decision rules, and deliverable expectations are available in a separate preamble window.

Questions to answer

  1. What is the cost and service implication of NovaCare’s current decentralized design?
  2. Which part families and regions, if any, should be aggregated at the NDC?
  3. When is full NDC centralization better than safety-stock-only pooling?
  4. How do CSL, CV, and regional demand correlation change the attractiveness of aggregation?
  5. What recommendation would you defend, and how sensitive is it to the assumptions?
